Frequently Asked Questions & Resources

What are your fees?

My fee for a 50-minute individual session is $180. For couples and family sessions (60 minutes), the fee is $220. I offer a limited number of sliding scale slots based on financial need. Please contact me to discuss further if finances are a barrier to seeking support.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes! Please contact me to check your insurance.

What should I expect in the first session?

The first session is an opportunity for us to get to know each other. We'll discuss your reasons for seeking therapy, your goals, and answer any questions you may have about the therapeutic process. It's a low-pressure way to see if we're a good fit and to begin building our therapeutic relationship.

How long does therapy last?

The duration of therapy varies greatly depending on your individual needs and goals. Some clients benefit from short-term, solution-focused therapy, while others may engage in longer-term work for deeper personal growth. We'll regularly check in on your progress and adjust our approach as needed.

Helpful Resources

Here are some organizations and resources that may offer additional support and information:

  • Mental Health America (MHA) - Provides advocacy, education, research, and services.
  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - Dedicated to building better lives for the millions of Americans affected by mental illness.
  • American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy (AAMFT) - Professional organization for MFTs.
  • American Psychological Association (APA) - Resources on various psychological topics.
